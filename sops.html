AI Workflow Audit
Standard Operating Procedure — A transferable, AI-assisted pipeline for auditing client workflows
This SOP documents the MIRJ Creative AI Workflow Audit pipeline. It is designed to be transferable, meaning any team member or collaborator can pick it up and run it with consistent quality. Each stage defines the human role, the AI role, the prompt logic to use, and the criteria that must be met before moving to the next stage.
Version 1.0 | MIRJ Creative | mirjcreative.com
How to Use This SOP
This document is a living template. Each time you run a new AI Workflow Audit engagement, duplicate this file, rename it with the client name and date, and work through the stages in order. Do not skip stages. Do not proceed past a stage until its hand-off criteria are met.

Key Conventions
Human tasks are marked with the role: Human.
AI tasks are marked with the role: AI (Claude).
Prompt logic sections contain the exact framing or instruction to give Claude at that stage.
Hand-off criteria are the checklist you complete before moving forward.
Stage 1 — Client Intake
1
Client Intake
Human RoleSend the intake form to the client. Review their responses before Claude processes them. Flag anything that is vague or missing.
AI RoleSummarize intake responses into a structured brief. Identify unanswered questions, vague responses, and potential workflow pain points.
Prompt Logic
"Summarize this client intake into a structured brief. Flag any questions that were left vague or unanswered. Identify the top 3 workflow pain points the client seems to be experiencing based on their responses."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Client has submitted all required fields
  • Claude summary brief is reviewed and approved by human
  • Any missing info has been followed up on
Stage 2 — Research and Context Building
2
Research and Context Building
Human RoleReview Claude's intake summary. Identify what additional context would make the audit sharper. Annotate or highlight anything that needs more investigation.
AI RoleResearch the client's industry workflow norms, common pain points in their tool category, and known limitations of the tools they listed. Produce a context brief.
Prompt Logic
"Based on this intake summary [paste summary], research the following: (1) common workflow pain points for [industry] teams of [team size], (2) known limitations or integration gaps in [list their tools], (3) how teams in this space are typically using AI today. Format your findings as a short context brief I can use to write an audit."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Context brief is complete
  • Human has read and annotated it
  • At least three specific, client-relevant insights are identified to anchor the audit
Stage 3 — Audit Draft Generation
3
Audit Draft Generation
Human RoleProvide Claude with the intake summary and context brief. Review the draft with a critical eye. Mark sections that feel off, generic, or that need more specificity.
AI RoleGenerate a structured audit document including a workflow assessment, tool stack analysis, identified gaps, and preliminary recommendations.
Prompt Logic
"Using the attached intake summary and context brief, write a first-pass AI Workflow Audit for [client name]. Structure it as: (1) Executive Summary, (2) Current Workflow Assessment, (3) Tool Stack Analysis, (4) Identified Gaps and Friction Points, (5) Preliminary Recommendations. Be specific to their context. Do not use generic filler. Flag anywhere you need more information with [NEEDS INFO]."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Draft exists in full
  • Every [NEEDS INFO] flag has been reviewed
  • Human has completed a first read and marked sections for revision
Stage 4 — Human Review and AI Refinement
4
Human Review and AI Refinement
Human RoleRead the full draft. Make strategic edits. Rewrite sections that do not reflect what you know about the client. Flag anything you want Claude to rephrase, expand, or tighten.
AI RoleCheck the draft for internal consistency, tone alignment, clarity, and completeness. Suggest rewrites for flagged sections. Do not change strategic direction without human instruction.
Prompt Logic
"Review this audit draft [paste draft] and do the following: (1) flag any sections that feel inconsistent or contradictory, (2) identify any recommendations that are too vague to act on, (3) rewrite the sections I have marked [REVISE] to be clearer and more specific. Do not change the strategic direction of any recommendation without asking me first."
Hand-off Criteria
  • All [REVISE] and [NEEDS INFO] flags are resolved
  • Recommendations are specific and actionable
  • Tone is consistent throughout
  • Human has signed off on the full draft
Stage 5 — Delivery Package Assembly
5
Delivery Package Assembly
Human RoleConfirm the final structure and format of the deliverable with the client before assembling. Prepare for the delivery conversation.
AI RoleFormat the final audit into a clean, client-ready document. Generate a one-page executive summary. Prepare a recommended next steps section.
Prompt Logic
"Take this final audit [paste draft] and do the following: (1) format it as a clean, professional document with clear section headers, (2) write a one-page executive summary for a non-technical stakeholder, (3) add a Next Steps section with 3 to 5 prioritized action items ranked by impact and ease of implementation."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Final document is formatted and proofread
  • Executive summary is complete
  • Next steps are prioritized and client-appropriate
  • Delivery is scheduled
Quick Reference — Prompt Starters by Stage
Stage 1 — Intake Summary
"Summarize this client intake into a structured brief. Flag vague or missing responses. Identify the top 3 workflow pain points."
Stage 2 — Research
"Research workflow norms and tool limitations for [industry] teams of [size] and produce a context brief."
Stage 3 — Draft
"Write a first-pass AI Workflow Audit for [client name] using the attached intake summary and context brief."
Stage 4 — Review
"Review this draft for consistency, clarity, and completeness. Rewrite flagged sections without changing strategic direction."
Stage 5 — Delivery
"Format the final audit, write a one-page executive summary, and add a prioritized next steps section."
Notes and Customization
This SOP is version 1.0. As you run audits with real clients, update this document with what you learn. Add new prompt logic that works. Flag stages that need more structure. This is designed to evolve.

Business Operations Audit
Standard Operating Procedure — A transferable, AI-assisted pipeline for auditing small business operations
This SOP documents the MIRJ Creative Business Operations Audit pipeline. Each stage defines the human role, the AI role, the prompt logic to use, and the criteria that must be met before moving to the next stage. Use alongside the Client Intake Form PDF, the Live Call Guide, and the Operations Audit Pipeline tool in this dashboard.
Version 1.0 | MIRJ Creative | mirjcreative.com
How to Use This SOP
Duplicate this file for each new engagement, rename it with the client name and date, and work through stages in order. Do not skip stages. Do not proceed until hand-off criteria are met.

Companion tools: Client Intake Form PDF (send before call), Live Call Guide (use during the call), Operations Audit Pipeline Tool (run each stage with one click).
Stage 1 — Client Intake Review
1
Client Intake Review
Human RoleSend the intake form PDF to the client at least 48 hours before the live call. Review responses and flag anything vague, missing, or inconsistent before Claude processes them.
AI RoleSummarize intake responses into a structured brief. Identify each friction area and whether it is a symptom or root cause. Flag gaps with [FOLLOW UP].
Prompt Logic
"Summarize this client intake into a structured brief with three sections: (1) Client Snapshot, (2) Friction Areas noting whether each is a symptom or root cause, (3) Gaps and Flags marked with [FOLLOW UP]."
Hand-off Criteria
  • All intake fields submitted
  • Claude summary brief reviewed and approved
  • Every [FOLLOW UP] resolved before the live call
  • Call prep notes complete
Stage 2 — Context and Research
2
Context and Research
Human RoleReview the intake summary. Identify what additional context would make the audit sharper. Note any industry knowledge Claude should factor in.
AI RoleResearch common operational breakdowns for this business type. Assess tool stack limitations. Identify likely root causes. Produce Audit Anchors to drive recommendations.
Prompt Logic
"Based on this intake summary [paste], produce a research context brief with: (1) Operational Patterns for Teams Like This, (2) Tool Stack Assessment, (3) Common Root Causes, (4) Audit Anchors — 3 to 4 specific insights that should drive recommendations."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Context brief complete and reviewed
  • At least three Audit Anchors identified
  • Industry knowledge noted for Stage 3
Stage 3 — Audit Draft Generation
3
Audit Draft Generation
Human RoleProvide Claude with both the intake summary and context brief. Review the draft critically. Mark sections that feel generic or need more specificity with [REVISE].
AI RoleGenerate a full audit document covering how work moves today, friction point analysis, tool gaps, and priority recommendations. Flag incomplete info with [NEEDS INFO] and judgment calls with [STRATEGIC CALL].
Prompt Logic
"Write a first-pass Business Operations Audit structured as: (1) Executive Summary, (2) How Work Moves Today, (3) Friction Point Analysis with symptom, root cause, and category for each, (4) Tool and System Gaps, (5) Priority Recommendations — 5 items by impact relative to effort. Flag [NEEDS INFO] and [STRATEGIC CALL]."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Draft complete across all five sections
  • Every [NEEDS INFO] reviewed
  • Every [STRATEGIC CALL] noted for human decision
  • First read complete with [REVISE] sections marked
Stage 4 — Human Review and AI Refinement
4
Human Review and AI Refinement
Human RoleRead the full draft. Make strategic edits. Rewrite anything that does not reflect what you know from the live call. Flag sections for Claude with [REVISE].
AI RoleCheck for consistency, tone, clarity, and completeness. Suggest rewrites for flagged sections. Do not change strategic direction without instruction.
Prompt Logic
"Review this audit draft and do: (1) Consistency Check — find contradictions and name duplications, (2) Tone Check — rewrite hedging sentences to be direct, show original and rewrite, (3) Recommendation Sharpness — rewrite any vague recommendation with what, who, and timeframe, (4) Consultant Handoff Notes — 4 to 5 bullets on what is strong and what to watch for in delivery."
Hand-off Criteria
  • All [REVISE] and [NEEDS INFO] flags resolved
  • All recommendations specific and actionable
  • Tone consistent and confident throughout
  • Human has signed off on the full draft
Stage 5 — Delivery Package Assembly
5
Delivery Package Assembly
Human RoleConfirm the final format with the client before assembling. Prepare your opening line for the delivery call. Review the next steps plan and adjust where your judgment calls for it.
AI RoleProduce a client-facing executive summary and a prioritized next steps plan with timelines and effort estimates. End with a suggested delivery call opener.
Prompt Logic
"Take this final audit and produce: (1) Client-Facing Executive Summary — 4 to 5 paragraphs for a non-technical business owner, warm and jargon-free, (2) Next Steps Plan — 5 items ranked by impact and ease, each with action, outcome, timeline, and lift level. End with one sentence the consultant can use to open the delivery call."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Executive summary complete and readable by a non-technical client
  • Next steps prioritized and client-appropriate
  • Delivery call opener ready
  • Delivery scheduled
Quick Reference — Prompt Starters by Stage
Stage 1 — Intake Review
"Summarize this client intake into a structured brief. Flag gaps with [FOLLOW UP]. Note whether each friction area is a symptom or root cause."
Stage 2 — Context and Research
"Produce a research context brief covering operational patterns, tool stack gaps, root causes, and 3 to 4 audit anchors."
Stage 3 — Audit Draft
"Write a first-pass Business Operations Audit for [client name] using the intake summary and context brief. Flag [NEEDS INFO] and [STRATEGIC CALL]."
Stage 4 — Review
"Review this draft for consistency, tone, and recommendation sharpness. Rewrite flagged sections without changing strategic direction."
Stage 5 — Delivery
"Produce a client-facing executive summary and a prioritized next steps plan with timelines and lift estimates. End with a delivery call opener."

Client Onboarding
Standard Operating Procedure — All three MIRJ Creative services
This SOP covers onboarding for the AI Workflow Audit, Alignment Reset, and AI Workflow Retainer. It documents every step from signed agreement to fully started engagement, with shared phases that apply to every client and service-specific branches where the experience diverges. Use this every time a client signs. Do not improvise the first 48 hours.
Version 1.0 | MIRJ Creative | mirjcreative.com
Phase Overview
Phase 1 — Confirm (Day 0): Universal. Confirm the engagement within 24 hours of signing.
Phase 2 — Set Up (Day 1): Universal. Internal setup before the client shows up.
Phase 3 — Launch (Day 2 to 3): Service-specific. Branches by audit, reset, or retainer.
Phase 4 — Hand Off (Day 3 to 5): Universal. Close the onboarding loop and move into the engagement.

Work through each phase in order. Do not move to the next phase until the checklist is complete.
Phase 1 — Confirm the Engagement
1
Confirm (Day 0 — within 24 hours of signing)
StepsSend the confirmation email within 2 hours of receiving the signed agreement. Log the client in Asana under the correct service pipeline. Create their Google Drive client folder. Set a follow-up reminder if you have not heard back within 24 hours.
Email Subject"You're confirmed — here's what comes next"
Body: Confirm receipt, share 2 to 3 service-specific bullets on what happens next, include your contact info.
Hand-off Criteria
  • Confirmation email sent within 2 hours
  • Client added to Asana with correct service tag
  • Google Drive folder created
  • Intake form or pre-work sent if applicable
Phase 2 — Set Up the Engagement
2
Set Up (Day 1 — within 24 to 48 hours of signing)
StepsBuild the Asana project from the correct service template. Set up all five Drive subfolders. Add any intake materials received. Review responses and prepare call notes. Confirm the first call and send a calendar invite with agenda. Add client to active tracker.
Drive Folders00 — Admin  |  01 — Intake and Pre-Work  |  02 — Session or Audit Notes  |  03 — Deliverables  |  04 — Next Steps
Hand-off Criteria
  • Asana project created from service template
  • All five Drive subfolders created
  • First call confirmed with calendar invite and agenda
  • Intake reviewed and call prep complete
  • Client added to active tracker
Phase 3 — Launch by Service
3
Launch (Day 2 to 3 — Service-specific)
AI Workflow AuditSend the intake form PDF. Set 48-hour return deadline. Schedule live audit call for day 5 to 7. Run Stage 1 of the Audit Pipeline tool. Share the Stage 1 brief. Confirm audit delivery date on the call.
Alignment ResetSend the pre-session questionnaire. Confirm all 3 session dates upfront. Share the Session 1 agenda. Create the shared working doc in Drive. Set expectations for between-session work. Confirm deliverable format before Session 1.
AI Workflow RetainerSchedule onboarding call within 48 hours. Share the retainer scope document. Set up recurring check-in calendar series. Create Asana project with monthly milestones. Establish communication channel and response SLA. Confirm the first deliverable and due date. Send the welcome packet.
Hand-off Criteria
  • Service-specific materials sent and received
  • First call or session scheduled and confirmed
  • Client has everything they need to show up prepared
  • Asana updated with service-specific milestones
Phase 4 — Hand Off to the Engagement
4
Hand Off (Day 3 to 5 — Universal)
StepsSend the orientation email with their Drive link, first call date, and how to reach you. Confirm client has access to their Drive folder. Make sure all pre-work is filed. Do a final Asana check. Self-check: if you disappeared tomorrow, could someone else pick this up?
Email Subject"You're all set — here's your quick reference"
Body: Drive link, call date and time, calendar link, best contact method.
Hand-off Criteria
  • Orientation email sent with Drive link and call date
  • Client confirmed access to Drive folder
  • All pre-work filed in the correct subfolder
  • Asana is clean, complete, and ready to track the engagement
  • You are prepared and the client feels it
Tools Reference
Email
Confirmation, orientation, and all client-facing onboarding communication
Google Drive
Client folder creation and file storage from day one — use the 5-subfolder structure every time
Asana
Internal project setup, task tracking, and milestone management — build from service template
Booking Link
First call scheduling — include in the confirmation email
Intake Form PDF
Sent to Audit clients within 24 hours of signing

AI Workflow Audit
Standard Operating Procedure — A transferable, AI-assisted pipeline for auditing client workflows
This SOP documents the MIRJ Creative AI Workflow Audit pipeline. It is designed to be transferable, meaning any team member or collaborator can pick it up and run it with consistent quality. Each stage defines the human role, the AI role, the prompt logic to use, and the criteria that must be met before moving to the next stage.
Version 1.0 | MIRJ Creative | mirjcreative.com
How to Use This SOP
This document is a living template. Each time you run a new AI Workflow Audit engagement, duplicate this file, rename it with the client name and date, and work through the stages in order. Do not skip stages. Do not proceed past a stage until its hand-off criteria are met.

Key Conventions
Human tasks are marked with the role: Human.
AI tasks are marked with the role: AI (Claude).
Prompt logic sections contain the exact framing or instruction to give Claude at that stage.
Hand-off criteria are the checklist you complete before moving forward.
Stage 1 — Client Intake
1
Client Intake
Human RoleSend the intake form to the client. Review their responses before Claude processes them. Flag anything that is vague or missing.
AI RoleSummarize intake responses into a structured brief. Identify unanswered questions, vague responses, and potential workflow pain points.
Prompt Logic
"Summarize this client intake into a structured brief. Flag any questions that were left vague or unanswered. Identify the top 3 workflow pain points the client seems to be experiencing based on their responses."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Client has submitted all required fields
  • Claude summary brief is reviewed and approved by human
  • Any missing info has been followed up on
Stage 2 — Research and Context Building
2
Research and Context Building
Human RoleReview Claude's intake summary. Identify what additional context would make the audit sharper. Annotate or highlight anything that needs more investigation.
AI RoleResearch the client's industry workflow norms, common pain points in their tool category, and known limitations of the tools they listed. Produce a context brief.
Prompt Logic
"Based on this intake summary [paste summary], research the following: (1) common workflow pain points for [industry] teams of [team size], (2) known limitations or integration gaps in [list their tools], (3) how teams in this space are typically using AI today. Format your findings as a short context brief I can use to write an audit."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Context brief is complete
  • Human has read and annotated it
  • At least three specific, client-relevant insights are identified to anchor the audit
Stage 3 — Audit Draft Generation
3
Audit Draft Generation
Human RoleProvide Claude with the intake summary and context brief. Review the draft with a critical eye. Mark sections that feel off, generic, or that need more specificity.
AI RoleGenerate a structured audit document including a workflow assessment, tool stack analysis, identified gaps, and preliminary recommendations.
Prompt Logic
"Using the attached intake summary and context brief, write a first-pass AI Workflow Audit for [client name]. Structure it as: (1) Executive Summary, (2) Current Workflow Assessment, (3) Tool Stack Analysis, (4) Identified Gaps and Friction Points, (5) Preliminary Recommendations. Be specific to their context. Do not use generic filler. Flag anywhere you need more information with [NEEDS INFO]."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Draft exists in full
  • Every [NEEDS INFO] flag has been reviewed
  • Human has completed a first read and marked sections for revision
Stage 4 — Human Review and AI Refinement
4
Human Review and AI Refinement
Human RoleRead the full draft. Make strategic edits. Rewrite sections that do not reflect what you know about the client. Flag anything you want Claude to rephrase, expand, or tighten.
AI RoleCheck the draft for internal consistency, tone alignment, clarity, and completeness. Suggest rewrites for flagged sections. Do not change strategic direction without human instruction.
Prompt Logic
"Review this audit draft [paste draft] and do the following: (1) flag any sections that feel inconsistent or contradictory, (2) identify any recommendations that are too vague to act on, (3) rewrite the sections I have marked [REVISE] to be clearer and more specific. Do not change the strategic direction of any recommendation without asking me first."
Hand-off Criteria
  • All [REVISE] and [NEEDS INFO] flags are resolved
  • Recommendations are specific and actionable
  • Tone is consistent throughout
  • Human has signed off on the full draft
Stage 5 — Delivery Package Assembly
5
Delivery Package Assembly
Human RoleConfirm the final structure and format of the deliverable with the client before assembling. Prepare for the delivery conversation.
AI RoleFormat the final audit into a clean, client-ready document. Generate a one-page executive summary. Prepare a recommended next steps section.
Prompt Logic
"Take this final audit [paste draft] and do the following: (1) format it as a clean, professional document with clear section headers, (2) write a one-page executive summary for a non-technical stakeholder, (3) add a Next Steps section with 3 to 5 prioritized action items ranked by impact and ease of implementation."
Hand-off Criteria
  • Final document is formatted and proofread
  • Executive summary is complete
  • Next steps are prioritized and client-appropriate
  • Delivery is scheduled
Quick Reference — Prompt Starters by Stage
Stage 1 — Intake Summary
"Summarize this client intake into a structured brief. Flag vague or missing responses. Identify the top 3 workflow pain points."
Stage 2 — Research
"Research workflow norms and tool limitations for [industry] teams of [size] and produce a context brief."
Stage 3 — Draft
"Write a first-pass AI Workflow Audit for [client name] using the attached intake summary and context brief."
Stage 4 — Review
"Review this draft for consistency, clarity, and completeness. Rewrite flagged sections without changing strategic direction."
Stage 5 — Delivery
"Format the final audit, write a one-page executive summary, and add a prioritized next steps section."
Notes and Customization
This SOP is version 1.0. As you run audits with real clients, update this document with what you learn. Add new prompt logic that works. Flag stages that need more structure. This is designed to evolve.
